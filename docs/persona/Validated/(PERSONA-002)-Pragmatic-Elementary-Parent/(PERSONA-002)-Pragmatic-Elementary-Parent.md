# David (Pragmatic Elementary Parent)

## Archetype Label

Data-Driven Realist

## Demographic Summary

Parent of elementary-aged children in South Portland. Works in a field with budget exposure (business, government, nonprofit management). Comfortable reading financial documents. Mid-30s to late 40s. May serve on a school committee or volunteer in an advisory capacity.

## Goals and Motivations

- Understand the budget holistically -- revenue sources, cost drivers, and trade-offs -- not just individual line items
- See evidence of sound fiscal management: reserves, multi-year projections, debt service ratios
- Support the school system with realistic expectations -- understands that cuts are sometimes necessary
- Wants the district to communicate trade-offs honestly rather than overselling or catastrophizing

## Frustrations and Pain Points

- Dislikes emotional appeals that substitute for data -- "think of the children" without showing the numbers
- Frustrated when budget presentations omit context (year-over-year trends, per-pupil comparisons, state funding changes)
- Finds it hard to get apples-to-apples comparisons because budget categories shift between years
- Wishes there were a single source of truth rather than scattered PDFs, presentations, and meeting minutes

## Behavioral Patterns

- Downloads the full proposed budget PDF. Reads the executive summary, then drills into specific funds.
- Builds his own spreadsheet to track trends. Annotates discrepancies or questions.
- Attends one or two key budget hearings but doesn't feel the need to speak at every meeting
- Shares observations privately with school board members he knows rather than in public comment

## Context of Use

Engages with budget data on a laptop, usually in the evening. Prefers structured data (tables, CSVs) over narrative. Would use an interactive tool if one existed. Trusts primary sources over summaries -- wants to verify claims against the actual budget document.
