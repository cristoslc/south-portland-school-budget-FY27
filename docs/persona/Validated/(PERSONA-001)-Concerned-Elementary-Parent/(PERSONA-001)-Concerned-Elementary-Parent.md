# Maria (Concerned Elementary Parent)

## Archetype Label

Vocal Parent Advocate

## Demographic Summary

Parent of one or more children currently in South Portland elementary schools (grades K-5). Engaged with the school community -- knows teachers by name, attends PTA meetings, follows school board agendas. Ranges from mid-20s to mid-40s. Moderate-to-high familiarity with school operations but limited understanding of municipal budgeting mechanics.

## Goals and Motivations

- Protect the quality of her children's day-to-day school experience: class sizes, specialist teachers (art, music, PE), support staff
- Ensure budget decisions don't disproportionately burden elementary programs
- Understand *where the money goes* so she can advocate effectively at public comment sessions
- Feel heard by decision-makers -- wants evidence that parent input shapes outcomes

## Frustrations and Pain Points

- Budget documents are opaque -- line items don't map to things she cares about (e.g., "how many classroom aides does this fund?")
- Feels blindsided by changes announced after decisions are effectively made
- Perceives that high school athletics and facilities get priority over elementary fundamentals
- Doesn't trust summary presentations -- wants to see the numbers herself but finds them hard to parse

## Behavioral Patterns

- Reads every school newsletter and budget FAQ. Cross-references claims with what she observes in her child's school.
- Shares budget info in parent group chats and neighborhood Facebook groups -- acts as an information multiplier
- Attends school board budget workshops. Prepares specific questions. Gets frustrated when answers are vague.
- Compares South Portland spending to neighboring districts (Scarborough, Cape Elizabeth) as a benchmark

## Context of Use

Engages with budget information primarily through school district communications, local news, and social media. Will read a detailed analysis if it's shared in a parent network. Consumes information on her phone during school pickup waits and after kids' bedtime. Values clear visualizations and plain-language summaries over raw spreadsheets.
