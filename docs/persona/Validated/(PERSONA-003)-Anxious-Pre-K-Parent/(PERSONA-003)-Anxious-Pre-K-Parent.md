# Jess (Anxious Pre-K Parent)

## Archetype Label

Future-Focused New Parent

## Demographic Summary

Parent of a toddler or preschooler (ages 1-4) who will enter South Portland schools within the next 1-3 years. May be new to the community or a long-time resident starting a family. Late 20s to late 30s. Limited familiarity with how the school district operates -- still learning which schools serve which neighborhoods.

## Goals and Motivations

- Understand what the school system will look like when her child enters kindergarten -- will full-day K still exist? Will her neighborhood school still be open?
- Evaluate whether South Portland schools are worth staying for or whether to consider other districts/private options
- Learn how early childhood programs (pre-K partnerships, developmental screenings) are funded
- Get a sense of trajectory -- is the district investing in growth or managing decline?

## Frustrations and Pain Points

- Feels like budget discussions are about the *current* system and don't address what's coming for younger kids
- Can't easily find information about how enrollment projections affect future resource allocation
- Overwhelmed by the volume of information at school board meetings -- doesn't know what's relevant to her situation yet
- Worried that decisions made now (staffing cuts, deferred maintenance, program elimination) will be irreversible by the time her child enrolls

## Behavioral Patterns

- Searches online for "South Portland schools quality" and "school ratings" -- budget details come later
- Asks other parents in playgroups and daycare what they've heard about the schools
- Follows local news and social media but rarely attends school board meetings (scheduling conflicts with young-child bedtimes)
- Would engage more if information were clearly targeted at incoming families

## Context of Use

Discovers budget information incidentally through social media, local news, or word of mouth. Consumes content on her phone in short bursts (nursing, nap time). Needs high-level takeaways first, with the option to drill deeper. Particularly responsive to "what this means for families entering the system" framing.
