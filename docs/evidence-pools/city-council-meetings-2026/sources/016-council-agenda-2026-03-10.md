A. ROLL CALL

B. WORKSHOP DISCUSSION

1) Update from METRO on Bus Routes/Future Service Expansion & Dissolution of the Transit Advisory Committee
   Two discussion points:
   - Providing feedback to Greater Portland Metro about various transit items
   - Whether to dissolve the City's Transit Advisory Committee

   Metro Update: Greater Portland Metro will provide an update on transit service following the 2024 merger.
   Key points:
   - Ridership stable at approximately 201,000 rides in 2025
   - New Scarborough route planned for summer 2026 along Route 1/Main Street corridor
   - Regional fare policy review in 2026, may include "tap-to-pay" fare technology
   - Cost-neutral scenario would restructure existing routes without additional City funding
   - Potential future enhancements (Maine Mall circulator, expanded microtransit) could require additional funds

   Metro seeking input on:
   - Whether Council supports more frequent and direct transit service
   - Initial reactions to draft route restructuring concepts
   - Guidance on balance between neighborhood coverage and faster routes
   - Interest in future service enhancements requiring additional City funding

   Dissolution of Transit Advisory Committee: Staff and existing members agree the committee's purpose is mostly obviated by City's representation on Metro board.

2) Temporary Emergency Rental Assistance
   Based on recent surge in federal immigration enforcement presence in Maine and resulting economic disruption, Mayor requested workshop on providing financial assistance to those struggling to pay rent.

   Two main options:
   Option 1: Provide Funding to a Nonprofit Organization
   Option 2: City-Administered Rental Assistance Program

   Staff recommends donation to nonprofits (Project Home, Cumberland County Homeless Prevention Program).
   Staff recommends no more than $150,000 from Undesignated Fund Balance.
   Average distribution by Project Home: ~$2,500 per household.

3) Bike Share Program/Ordinance
   Proposed limited one-year pilot: ~40 bikes across 8 stations.
   Funding: MaineDOT ($100,000) + municipal match ($25,000).
   Third-party vendor operation, similar to Portland's program.

   Council asked to provide direction on:
   - Bikeshare regulatory framework adequacy
   - Whether to direct staff to proceed with drafting a municipal bikeshare ordinance

4) E-Bikes on Greenbelt / Bikes on Sidewalks

   E-Bikes on Greenbelt:
   - Current interpretation: e-bikes prohibited (not actively enforced)
   - Staff proposing to align City definitions with State definitions
   - Class 1 and Class 2 e-bikes would be allowed
   - Full Greenbelt replacement estimated at $6.5 million due to erosion, tree roots, pavement damage

   Bikes on Sidewalks:
   - Previously discussed September 9, 2025; Council deferred to before summer 2026
   - Public Works Director requests e-bikes not be allowed on sidewalks
   - Biking restricted in areas with outdoor dining (e.g., Mill Creek)
   - Police not actively monitoring sidewalks for bicycle use currently
