Norms for School Board Members
● Active Listening:Paying attention to what others are saying and showing that you are
engaged, even when you disagree. This includes asking clarifying questions and avoiding
interrupting.
● Acknowledging Contributions: This may include recognizing others’ work, ideas, efforts
and contributions.
● Showing Empathy: Attempting to be mindful of the feelings, views, experiences and
opinions of others, even if you don’t agree with their perspective.
● Attempting to be mindful of the value of every individual, regardless of their background,
experiences, beliefs, or actions.
● Civility. Attempting to be mindful of others’ boundaries and privacy, and treating them with
kindness.
● Avoiding Premature Judgment. Refraining from making judgments or assumptions about
others based on limited information or stereotypes.

South Portland District Goals 25-26
● Attendance 3% increase in number of students with attendance rate of 90% or
above
● Belonging 5% increase in the percentage of students who report a sense of
belonging at school
● Achievement
○ 72% of students will be at or above the 41st percentile on the NWEA
Math test
○ 68% of students will be at or above the 41st percentile on the NWEA
Reading test.
● Equity
○ Close the achievement gap of identified subgroups by 5% in Math &
Reading.
9

South Portland Equity Commitments 25-26
We
● Believe in the unlimited capacity of all students, and hold all students to high
expectations
● Use data to drive equity and close opportunity gaps
● Use our resources, especially time, with urgency and intentionality in pursuit
of excellence for all students
● Embrace our responsibility and capacity to make a difference in our students’
lives
● Commit to interrupting injustice, and take action for equity

Our Administrators Guiding Principles for FY27
● We want to use the feedback from last year's budget season to improve our
process
● South Portland School District embraces the Community School Strategy as
the foundation of the way we design our programs. This model enhances
learning, and engages family and community partners to meet the diverse
needs of all learners.
● Our decisions reflect a commitment to outcomes for students furthest from
opportunity
● Staff in all roles are vital to achieving our mission, and our labor contracts
demonstrate our commitment to SPSD Staff
● Taxpayers deserve a school district that is both educationally effective and
fiscally responsible.

Next Steps
● We want to ensure a bright future in South Portland, and while reductions
will be challenging, want to explore some potential larger scale ideas for
savings
● We are working on cost analysis for a few big ideas, and welcome more
ideas for exploration
● In January we will hold a Community Forum where all are invited to engage
in learning more about these ideas, and engage in dialog with the
Administrative Team.
● The Administrative Team will work on advising and preparing the
Superintendent's Budget, using feedback from surveys and Community
Forum.
27

Next Steps
● February 9 the Superintendent will share a summary of the team’s proposal
with the Board and community. It is anticipated that this summary will
include some options for consideration.
● The Board will then facilitate workshops to consider the proposal, and then
adopt the next version of the budget - the Board Budget which will be
shared with Council.
28

Ideas for Exploration & Analysis
1. Redesign our elementary school grade level configurations to
provide at least one early learning campus to meet the needs of
PreK and Child Development Services (CDS)
2. Review viability for SPMS to use a single start and end time
3. Review high school and adult education programming for
expanded access, alternative pathways, and redesigned
schedules
4. Explore shift changes and other creative ideas to realize
operational efficiencies
And what other ideas could we explore?
