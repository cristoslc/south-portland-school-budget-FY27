## Slide 5

Core Ground Rules for Effective Dialogue
● Active Listening and Presence: Listen to understand, not just to respond.
Pay attention to nonverbal cues and avoid interrupting.
● Share the Air: Ensure everyone has an opportunity to speak, and be conscious of not
dominating the conversation.
● Respectful Interaction: Treat others with respect, even when holding opposing views.
Avoid personal attacks, name-calling, and stereotyping.
● Speak for Yourself ("I" Statements): Share your own experiences, perspectives, and
feelings, rather than generalizing or speaking for others.
● Focus on Understanding, Not Winning: Approach the conversation with an open
mind, aiming to learn why others hold their views.
● Challenge Ideas, Not People: Critically discuss concepts, but treat people with dignity.

December Ideas for Exploration & Analysis
1. Redesign our elementary school grade level configurations to
provide at least one early learning campus to meet the needs of
PreK and Child Development Services (CDS)
2. Review viability for SPMS to use a single start and end time
3. Review high school and adult education programming for
expanded access, alternative pathways, and redesigned
schedules
4. Explore shift changes and other creative ideas to realize
operational efficiencies
