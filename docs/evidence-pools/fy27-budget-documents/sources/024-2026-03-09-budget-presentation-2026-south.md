Revenue - Fund Balance
● FY27 and beyond are critical years to rebuilding fund balance.
○ City of South Portland’s policy: minimum 9% unassigned fund balance, maximum 12%.
○ The Board will need to develop new fiscal policies around fund balance thresholds and where
appropriate: use for operations or offsetting tax increases.
● The Leadership Team Recommends: Any further reductions to the proposed
FY27 budget should not be replaced with other items, in order to re-establish
emergency savings.
○ For Instance - if Health Insurance increase comes back at 11% instead of
12%, we urge the board to not add back expenses, but instead support
contingency planning and the rebuilding of fund balance in FY27.

Summary
31

FY 27 and Beyond
The Leadership Team has crafted a budget that
● Respects the budget constraint of no more than 6% increase to taxpayers
● Includes a 3.3% increase over FY26, the lowest increase in 5 years (4.8% to
10.7% over the last five years)
● Will support outcomes from students, and allow the district to meet its legal
obligations for programming and supports
● Will put South Portland on a path to financial sustainability, given our
negotiated labor contracts and debt service, we will continue to face
challenges to keep costs below the contract rates, but this year will be an
important step forward
32

Looking Forward
The Leadership Team
● Is hopeful The Board and stakeholders will understand the level of analysis
that has been undertaken by the team of 25 leaders in the district
● Has more than 500 years combined working in education, and have expertise
in their fields
● Is ready to move forward with care and kindness to support the community,
especially those whose positions are eliminated, in the transition
● Respects the Budget Process, and knows the sooner the Board is able to
adopt a budget, it will allow us to move forward with planning and
implementation of needed change.
