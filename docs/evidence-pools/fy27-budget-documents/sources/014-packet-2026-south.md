Budget Workshop I
Chair DeAngelis shared upcoming important dates:
● Board Workshop: Monday, December 22, 2025 5:30 p.m. SPHS Learning Commons
● Citizens School Budget Forum: Thursday, January 22, 2026, more details to follow
Dr. Prince, Assistant Superintendent, shared that the budget webpage for FY27 is on the district website at
www.spsdme.org/budget27. Budge-related materials will be available here for reference.
Dr. Prince shared a presentation to provide context to new board members and refresh existing board members'
memories on the budget process. District leaders will be working to support the creation of the Superintendent’s
Budget.
The objectives of the Workshop include:
● Understand the demographic and financial context of the South Portland School Department.
● Provide financial guidance for the Superintendent on the first draft of FY27 Budget.
● Begin engagement on big ideas for long term financial sustainability, and indicate bright spots for more
exploration.
Timeline for the budget process:
● December: Stakeholder input
● January: Superintendent budget development
● February: Superintendent budget recommendation to Board
● March: School Board Workshop and community input
● April: City Council presentation
● May: City Council vote
● June: public vote

BOARD OF EDUCATION
Guiding Priorities for the Superintendent’s Budget Development:
● Attendance: 3% increase in the number of students with attendance rate of 90% or above.
● Belonging: 5% increase in the percentage of students who report a sense of belonging at school.
● Achievement: 72% of students will be at or above the 41st percentile on the NWEA Math test. 68% of
students will be at or above the 41st percentile on the NWEA Reading test.
● Equity: Close the achievement gap of identified subgroups by 5% in Math and Reading.
Administrators Guiding Principals for FY27 Budget Process:
● We want to use the feedback from last year’s budget season to improve our process.
● South Portland School District embraces the Community School Strategy as the foundation of the way we
design our programs. This model enhances learning, engages family and community partners to meet
diverse needs of all learners.
● Our decisions reflect a commitment to outcomes for students furthest from opportunity.
● Staff in all roles are vital to achieving our mission, and our labor contracts demonstrate our commitment to
SPSD staff.
● Taxpayers deserve a school district that is both educationally effective and fiscally responsible.
Dr. Prince reported on enrollment and staffing changes over the last several years.
Dr. Prince shared a budget forecast with different options for FY27.
Dr. Prince shared next steps, including:
● Exploring potential larger scale ideas for savings.
● Working on a cost analysis for a few big ideas.
● Holding a Community Forum where all are invited to engage in learning about these ideas and engage in
dialog with the Administrative Team.
● The Administrative Team working on advising and preparing the Superintendent’s Budget, using feedback
from surveys and the Community Forum.
Ideas for Exploration and Analysis:
● Redesign elementary school grade level configurations to provide at least one early learning campus to
meet the needs of PreK and Child Development Services (CDS).
● Review viability for SPMS to use a single start and end time.
● Review SPHS and Adult Education programming for expanded access, alternative pathways, and
redesigned schedules.
● Explore shift changes and other creative ideas to realize operational efficiencies.
Board Direction to Superintendent:
● What tax increase is the Board willing to support as the assumption for drafting the Superintendent’s
budget?
● What are the big ideas the Board would recommend for further modeling?
After discussion, the Board recommended to the Superintendent a tax increase between Options A and B
(3.19-9.73%) presented at the Workshop, which is 6.5%.

Board, as an elected representative body of the school unit, wishes to provide opportunity for
citizens the public to express their views interests and concerns related to the matters under
consideration by the Board. The public is invited to cordially invited to attend and participate
as set forth in this policy.
Board meetings are conducted to carry for the purpose of carrying out the official business of the
School Department. The meetings are not public forum meetings (as are town meetings), but are
meetings which are held for the Board (as it is required by law) to do to conduct its business in
public. The minutes of each public meeting will record the action taken and will show how the
Board voted on each item presented for action. The journal of minutes is open and available to
the public during normal business hours in the Office of the Superintendent of Schools Office
and on the district website.
Orderly conduct of a meeting does not permit spontaneous discussion from the audience nor
among Board members. Individuals or organizations desiring to make requests, presentations, or
proposals on matters before the Board will be provided that opportunity with prior arrangements.
Generally, oOpportunities for public participation comment will be provided in two places on
the agenda. The public may speak on items on or off the agenda and may speak at one but not
both times. Opportunities to speak on agenda items will take place prior to any vote. the
Board’s vote on those items. Comments on items not on the agenda will take place prior to
adjournment.
The intent of this policy is to allow a fair and adequate opportunity for the public to be heard, to
provide adequate time for the Board to obtain necessary information on a subject before it, and
to see that time allowed for open discussion does not interfere with the fulfillment of the
scheduled agenda of the Board.
An agenda shall be published in advance of each meeting in accordance with Board policy.
Copies are to be posted, or available at the Superintendent’s office, on the district website, and
at the meeting. in each school, city hall, the public library or other appropriate public facilities.
Anyone desiring additional information about any item on the agenda should direct such
inquiries to the Office of the Superintendent.
The following “ground rules” are to further guide public comment participation at meetings:
A. All speakers will provide their name and town/city address before speaking. are to
identify themselves as they begin talking. They will not be permitted to participate in
gossip, make defamatory comments, or use abusive or vulgar language. The Board Chair
will maintain the prerogative to discontinue any presentation which violates any of the

public participation guidelines.
B. Orderly conduct of a meeting does not permit spontaneous discussion from the audience
nor among Board members.
B. No complaints or allegations will be allowed concerning any personnel or any person
connected to the school system. If appropriate, concerns about an individual will be
handled in a conference where the rights and interests of all parties will be appropriately
regarded.
C. All speakers are to address the Board Chair and may direct questions or comments to
Board Members or other officers of the school system only upon approval of the chair.
Members of the Board and the Superintendent have the privilege of asking questions of
any person who addresses the Board. Such questions must be addressed through the
chair.
D. Each speaker is given three minutes for comments. The Chair may limit the time
given to comments on a particular topic as well as the time any individual may speak.
E. In the event of a sizable audience, tThe chair may require persons interested in speaking to
so indicate by signing up. to speak, so they may be called on in the most expedient order.
F. Comments and suggestions will be welcomed and given consideration by the Board.
Speakers may offer objective comments on school operations and programs that concern
them. Generally, the Board may hear but not discuss or act on an item not on the agenda.
Personnel and/or personal matters or complaints will not be entertained in a public
meeting but will be deferred to established resolution procedures.
G. Citizens, employees, and others with a legitimate interest in the Board’s business are
welcome to participate as provided in this policy. Others may be recognized to speak at
the Board’s discretion. Employees or employee groups will not be permitted to discuss
matters for which other, more appropriate forums, are provided.
G. H. Generally, duplication or repetition of comments to the Board should be avoided in
order to make the most efficient use of the time in meetings. Groups or organizations are
requested to be represented by designated spokespersons.
H. All questions will be recorded by the Superintendent and answered, to the extent
possible, at the end of public comment. If answers are not available, someone will
be assigned to reply to you as soon as the information is available.
I. The statement read at the time of Public Comment will be as follows: a. The Board’s
Public Comment is an opportunity for the Board to listen to opinions of the public
that relate to school and education matters on or off this evening’s agenda. The
Board is here to listen. Anyone wishing to speak will be asked to proceed to the

microphone and state your name and town/city where you reside. You will be given
three minutes to speak. As a reminder, School Board meetings are broadcast live,
recorded, and posted on the city’s website. Please consider the impact of your
comments.
