## Slide 21

Special Education Students
Development of Transition Programming:
➢ The State of Maine has passed legislation in 2023 that requires public schools to provide special education and related services to eligible
students until they turn 22 or receive a regular high school diploma, whichever comes first.
○ Prior to 2023 we had responsibilities until age 20.
○ Implications have required expanding programming for students with disabilities preparing them for adult life. Instructional areas
include - employment skills, independent living, community access, and postsecondary education or training
■ Students participate in community-based instruction, internships, job coaching, self-advocacy development, and
functional life skills practice aligned to their IEP transition goals
■ The idea is that program shifts from primarily academic and school related instruction to real-world application,
emphasizing independence, dignity, and meaningful adult outcomes
New Programming for 2026:
➢ The State of Maine has passed legislation that requires public schools to be responsible for the special education needs of 3 and 4 year
olds
■ Our department will take responsibility for serving 4 year olds who starting September 1, 2026
■ Additional students that we will be serving is likely to be between 80-90 children for next school year
■ Our Department will take responsibility for serving 3 year olds in FY28
■ Programming for this age group is different philosophically than it is for school aged-students. It is more
developmentally focused, and centered on foundational skills such as communication, social interaction,
sensory/emotional regulation, and early motor development, often delivered through embedded routines and natural
environments.

## Slide 34

Norms for School Board Members
● Active Listening: Paying attention to what others are saying and showing
that you are engaged, even when you disagree. This includes asking
clarifying questions and avoiding interrupting.
● Acknowledging Contributions: This may include recognizing others’ work,
ideas, efforts and contributions.
● Showing Empathy: Attempting to be mindful of the feelings, views,
experiences and opinions of others, even if you don’t agree with their
perspective.
● Attempting to be mindful of the value of every individual, regardless of
their background, experiences, beliefs, or actions.
● Civility. Attempting to be mindful of others’ boundaries and privacy, and
treating them with kindness.
● Avoiding Premature Judgment. Refraining from making judgments or
assumptions about others based on limited information or stereotypes.
