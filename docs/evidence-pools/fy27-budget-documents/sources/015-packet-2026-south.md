Good evening members of the School Board, administrators, staff, and community members.
Thank you for the opportunity to share updates from across our schools.
At South Portland High School, students have shown strong leadership and care for their
community through a new student-led initiative called The Little Kitchen. This project was
created to support families and neighbors who were unable to leave their homes during recent
weather and community challenges. Located near the cafeteria and lecture hall, the Little
Kitchen provides food and essential items for anyone in need. Students organized donations,
set up the space, and encouraged others to take items for themselves or deliver them to
neighbors. This initiative reflects the compassion and responsibility our students continue to
show for one another and the broader community.
Last week, the high school also hosted a Black History Month student panel in celebration of
Black History Month. Students shared personal experiences, discussed identity and history, and
engaged in meaningful conversation about culture and leadership. The event created space for
reflection, celebration, and learning across the school community.
Looking ahead, students are preparing for the first-ever SPHS Co-Ed Futsal World Cup
Tournament, taking place after school during the week of March 9 through March 13. The
championship game will be played during Community Week on April 16 in front of the entire
school. Teams of five mixed-gender students may sign up, and donations of up to twenty-five
dollars per team are appreciated but not required, as the event will raise funds for the senior
class. Winter athletics are also wrapping up, with many teams heading into postseason
competition and continuing to represent the school with pride.
At South Portland Middle School, students have made strong academic gains in reading and
math based on winter assessment data, particularly those receiving additional intervention
support. The school will host its Shark Team Learning Night on February 10 to help families
learn ways to support students at home. Winter athletics have been active, with basketball
finishing, volleyball beginning, and indoor track, swimming, and unified basketball underway.
At Kaler Elementary School, students have been learning about Olympic events and created a
collaborative bulletin board celebrating teamwork and perseverance. At Brown School, students
are sharing messages about what they love about their school and will celebrate a schoolwide
reading incentive later this month. Dyer Elementary has launched a Friendship Festival focused
on kindness, community helpers, and school spirit. Teachers across the elementary level
continue to use professional development time to strengthen small-group instruction and
support student learning.
South Portland Adult Education has spent the past month responding to the recent increase in
immigration enforcement in the area. In response to student input, English language acquisition
classes were moved to remote instruction using WhatsApp and will remain remote through
February 13. Adult Education continues working on plans to strengthen remote learning options
in case in-person instruction is disrupted again. Access to technology remains a need, and

thanks to support from the district and community partners, 100 reliable low-cost Chromebooks
were secured to support consistent learning. Three additional students passed the HiSET this
month, and the program has now doubled its number of HiSET graduates compared to last year
at this time.
Across the district, we continue to see growth, resilience, and strong community engagement.
Thank you for your continued support of our students and schools.

Public Participation At Board Meetings
DRAFT
Regular, special and emergency meetings of the School Board are open to the public. This
Board, as an elected representative body of the school unit, wishes to provide opportunity for the
public citizens to express its views interests and concerns related to the matters under
consideration by the Board. The public is invited to cordially invited to attend and participate as
set forth in this policy. Everyone in the community is invited. Board policies are available in
multiple languages on the district website.
Board meetings are conducted to carry for the purpose of carrying out the official business of the
School Department. The meetings are not public forum meetings (as are town meetings), but are
meetings which are held for the Board to do its business in public. The minutes of each public
meeting will record the action taken and will show how the Board voted on each item presented
for action. The journal of minutes is open and available to the public during normal business
hours in the Office of the Superintendent of Schools and on the district website.
Orderly conduct of a meeting does not permit spontaneous discussion from the audience nor
among Board members. Individuals or organizations desiring to make requests, presentations or
proposals on matters before the Board should make prior arrangements through the chair. will be
provided that opportunity with prior arrangements.
Generally, Opportunities for public comment participation will be provided in two places on the
agenda. The public may speak on items on or off the agenda (related to school/education) at
either or both comment periods. and may speak at one but not both times. Opportunities to speak
on agenda items will take place prior to any vote. There will be no interaction between the
public/public comment and members of the Board. the Board’s vote on those items. Comments
on items not on the agenda will take place prior to adjournment.
The intent of this policy is to allow a fair and adequate opportunity for the public to be heard, to
provide adequate time for the Board to obtain necessary information on a subject before it, and
to see that time allowed for open discussion does not interfere with the fulfillment of the
scheduled agenda of the Board.
An agenda shall be published in advance of each meeting in accordance with Board policy.
Copies are to be posted, or available at the Superintendent’s office and on the district website and
at the meeting. in each school, city hall, the public library or other appropriate public facilities.
Anyone desiring additional information about any item on the agenda should direct such
inquiries to the Office of the Superintendent.
The following “ground rules” are to further guide public comment participation at meetings:
A. All speakers will provide their name and town/city address before speaking. are to
identify themselves as they begin talking. They will not be permitted to participate in
gossip, make defamatory comments, or use abusive or vulgar language. The Board Chair

will maintain the prerogative to discontinue any presentation which violates any of the
public participation guidelines.
B. No complaints or allegations will be allowed concerning any personnel or any person
connected to the school system. If appropriate, concerns about an individual will be
handled in a conference where the rights and interests of all parties will be appropriately
regarded. Orderly conduct of a meeting does not permit spontaneous discussion from the
audience or among Board members.
C. All speakers are to address the Board Chair and may direct questions or comments to
Board Members or other officers of the school system only upon approval of the chair.
Members of the Board and the Superintendent have the privilege of asking questions of
any person who addresses the Board. Such questions must be addressed through the
chair.
D. Each speaker is given 3 minutes for comments. The Chair may limit the time given to
comments on a particular topic as well as the time any individual may speak.
E. In the event of a sizable audience,The Chair may require persons interested in speaking
to so indicate by signing up to speak, so they may be called on in the most expedient
order.
F. Comments and suggestions will be welcomed and given consideration by the Board.
Speakers may offer objective comments on school operations and programs that concern
them. Generally, the Board may hear but not discuss or act on an item not on the agenda.
Personnel and/or personal matters or complaints will not be entertained in a public
meeting but will be deferred to established resolution procedures.
G. Citizens, employees, and others with a legitimate interest in the Board’s business are
welcome to participate as provided in this policy. Others may be recognized to speak at
the Board’s discretion. Employees or employee groups will not be permitted to discuss
matters for which other, more appropriate forums, are provided.
G. Generally, duplication or repetition of comments to the Board should be avoided in
order to make the most efficient use of the time in meetings. Groups or organizations are
requested to be represented by designated spokespersons.
H. All questions will be recorded by the Superintendent and answered, to the extent
possible, at the end of public comment. If answers are not available, someone will be
assigned to reply to you as soon as the information is available.
I. The statement read at the time of Public Comment Open Discussion will be as follows:

community stability, even where our direct regulatory authority is limited;

experienced by residents because of increased federal immigration enforcement
activity within the community and affirms its commitment to the safety and dignity
of all who live and work in South Portland.
2. The City Council urges federal immigration authorities operating within South
Portland to conduct any enforcement activities in a manner that prioritizes
transparency, clear identification, and de-escalation, and that minimizes
unnecessary fear or disruption to the public.
3. The City Council respectfully requests that federal authorities provide clear
communication to local officials regarding the nature, scope, and duration of
enforcement activity occurring within the City, to the extent permitted by law, so
that local leaders can appropriately address community concerns and ensure
public safety.

employees will continue to operate in accordance with state and local law and will
not engage in federal immigration enforcement activities except as legally
required.

public information regarding available community resources related to their
rights, and appropriate points of contact for public safety concerns, in
coordination with trusted local and state organizations where appropriate.

leadership to continue to seek greater transparency, oversight, and
accountability regarding federal immigration enforcement practices impacting
Maine communities.
7. The City Council directs the City Manager to communicate with Governor Janet
Mills and appropriate State of Maine officials to request consideration of a
temporary, statewide 60-day moratorium on residential evictions for households
that have experienced substantial financial hardship associated with the recent
surge in federal immigration enforcement activity.
8. This resolution is adopted as an expression of the South Portland City Council’s
concern for public safety and community well-being and does not purport to
regulate or restrict federal authority, but rather to formally communicate the
City’s position and the impacts being experienced locally.
